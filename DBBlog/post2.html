Joining Forces with University College London

November 15, 2021

University College London (UCL) have provided their world-leading research expertise to help DesignBuilder enhance our tools for existing building performance assessment. The partnership is developing a new set of `in-use’ performance evaluation tools that go way beyond current industry norms to help our users reduce the environmental impact of their buildings. Some of these capabilities (such as Uncertainty and Sensitivity Analysis) plus enhancements to our Optimisation and Results Viewer tools were released in recent versions. More tools to help streamline the model calibration process will follow over the next year, building on the work we did helping to develop CIBSE TM63.

DesignBuilder director David Cocking explains: “Our vision is to help our customers significantly reduce the impact of their buildings on the planet whilst maximising occupant wellbeing. Our partnership with UCL has given us the technical expertise and knowledge to develop these innovative new tools, placing DesignBuilder at the forefront of research in this emerging field.”

UCLs project lead, Prof. Dejan Mumovic, Director of the Institute for Environmental Design and Engineering is keen to underline the mutually-beneficial aspects of the partnership: “… this partnership is also allowing us to demonstrate the practical application of our research into the operational performance of buildings. The new DesignBuilder software tools are already being used as part of our teaching and have also formed part of research published in several journal articles and conference papers.”

As highlighted on the “Cities, Regions & Built Environment Day” at COP26, there is a critical need to improve the performance of the existing building stock. The new tools developed as part of this partnership will help address this need, and will further strengthen DesignBuilder’s position at the forefront of building performance simulation.
