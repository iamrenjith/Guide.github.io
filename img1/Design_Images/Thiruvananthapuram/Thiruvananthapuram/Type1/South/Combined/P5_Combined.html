Parallel Coordinates plot

Parallel Coordinates Plots is type of data visualisation method. They are ideal for comparing many variables together and seeing the relationships between them. In this context, each variable (design parameter) is given its own axis. Each axis is placed parallel to each other and have different scales -- depending on their units. The values are plotted as a series of lines that are interconnected between axes. These interconnections show the relationship between them to produce the corresponding thermal and daylight performance. The variables or the design parameters were selected based on a sensitivity analysis. For the sensitivity analysis, a number of design parameters including of Window-Wall ratio (WWR) for different facades, U-values for walls, windows, roof and floor, reflectance of wall, ceiling, floor, outside ground etc. were considered. For sampling from among these parameters, Morris method was adopted. From the results of the analysis, the most sensitive parameters were selected and were used to produce the parallel coordinates plots.

Terminologies

Thermal performance - Energy demand

The thermal performance of the buildings were assessed by carrying out building performance simulations using EnergyPlus v8.9 software. In this study, the whole building was assumed as a single thermal zone. The HVAC template in the model was defined using Packaged Terminal Air Conditioner (PTAC) where as ventilation was defined using airflow network. The thermostat set point temperatures for heating and cooling were defined based on adaptive thermal comfort model. This was because the latest version of the Nation Building Code of India (NBC) had adopted the Indian Model of Adaptive Comport (IMAC) for specifying the comfort conditions inside the building. According to the IMAC model, for mixed-mode ventilated buildings the neutral/comfort temperatures are calculated using the equation:
T_c=0.28T_o+17.9
where T_c is the neutral/comfort temperature in degree Celsius and T_o is the 30-day outdoor running mean air temperature ranging from 13°C to 38.5°C . The limits of 90% acceptability are ±3.5°C. The upper and lower limits of 90% acceptability comfort/neutral temperature were calculated using IMAC model and were used as set point temperatures for cooling and heating respectively.
The building was modelled to have mixed mode ventilation and the ventilation was controlled by opening the windows (at an angle of 90°) when both mean zone and outside air temperature were within the IMAC acceptability comfort limits (90%). The buildings were also modelled to have ceiling fans which were functional when the indoor zone temperature was above 25°C and when the HVAC was not ON. The use of ceiling fans is considered as one of the main mechanisms in Warm-Humid climates to improve the indoor thermal comfort. When ceiling fans were in operation the cooling set points (i.e. IMAC 90% acceptability upper limit) were offset by 2.2°C. Energy Management System (EMS) programs were written to define the ventilation and ceiling operation schedules. For assessing the building performances, only annual heating and cooling energy demands were considered. More details will be provided in the design report.

Daylight performance - Useful Daylight Illuminance - combined

A number of Radiance-based methods are available to perform Climate-Based Daylight Modelling (CBDM). The simulations were run with 2-Phase method. Since the study does not involve complex glazing systems, 2-Phase method (with higher sky resolution) was used to assess the daylight performance of buildings.
The climate-based metric Useful Daylight Illuminance (UDI) was used to assess daylighting performance. The simulations were run with 2-Phase method. UDI was calculated in terms of UDI not achieved (UDI-n), UDI combined (UDI-c) and UDI exceeded (UDI-e).

  • UDI-n was calculated as the percentage of annual occupied hours for which the illuminance values are less than 100 lux.
  • UDI-c was calculated as the percentage of annual occupied hours for which the illuminance values falls between 100 lux and 3000 lux and
  • UDI-e was calculated as the percentage of annual occupied hours for which the illuminance values are greater than 3000 lux.

The time schedule 08:00-17:00 (without considering daylight saving time) was used as occupancy period. The sensor grid points were placed at a (horizontal) working plane height of 0.8 m above the floor level and 0.25 m from the surrounding walls. Results from each UDI range were obtained for all sensor points on the working plane, and then averaged.

U-values (W/m²K)

U-value is the measure of the heat transmission through a material (or a combination of materials). The U-values are expressed in W/m²K. Lower the U-value, better the insulation. For development of this design guide, the U-values for walls, windows, roof and floor were considered. The U-values for walls and windows were considered separately for the different facades. The design parameters (with U-values) provided in the parallel coordinates plot are only for the sensitive ones. For the other facades, the designer can choose materials his/her desired/preferred U-values. Some of the common material and their U-values are provided in the Energy Conservation Building Code of India.

Window-Wall Ratio (WWR)

Window-Wall Ratio maybe defined as the ratio of total area of window to the total area of the exterior wall. For the simulations, the WWR were varied separately for different facades. So, the WWR in this context maybe defined as the ratio of total area of windows in a particular facade to the total area of exterior wall in that facade.

Reflectance (%)

The reflectance of a material is the ability of the material to reflect the energy incident on its surface. The reflectance of a material is the ratio of the light reflected to the light incident. This value can be used to describe the nature of a surface in terms of the surface finish. The reflectance values is presented in terms of percentages (%).

Transmittance (%)

The transmittance is the ratio of the light passing through to the light incident on the material. The transmittance values is presented in terms of percentages (%).
